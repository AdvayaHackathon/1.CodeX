Cultural Etiquette Guide

Cultural Etiquette in India

Greetings

In India, the traditional greeting is "Namaste" with palms pressed together in front of the chest and a slight bow. Handshakes are also common in business settings. Remove shoes before entering homes or places of worship.

Dining

In traditional settings, food may be eaten with your right hand only. Left hands are considered unclean. Wait to be seated and served. It's polite to try everything offered. Always express appreciation for the food.

Dress Code

Dress modestly, especially at religious sites where shoulders and knees should be covered. Remove shoes before entering temples, mosques, and homes. Wearing bright colors is welcome and common.

Religious Customs

India is diverse with many religions. Always be respectful at places of worship. Don't touch or point at religious statues or images. Photography may be restricted in certain areas of temples and mosques.

Gift Giving

Give and receive gifts with both hands. Avoid gifts of leather to Hindus or alcohol/meat items to Muslims. Gifts are often opened in private, not in front of the giver. Sweets are always a safe gift option.

Taboos

Avoid public displays of affection. Don't point the soles of your feet toward people or religious objects. Avoid touching someone's head, as it's considered sacred. Ask before taking photos of people.
